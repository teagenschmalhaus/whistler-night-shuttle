# Website Design Inspiration

Reference layouts we like for the Whistler Night Shuttle website.

---

## 1. Transfeero — The Scale + Simplicity Play
**URL:** https://www.transfeero.com/en/

### Why we like it
Clean, minimal layout that communicates reliability and professionalism instantly. The design gets out of the way and lets the booking form do the work.

### Key layout elements
- **Hero:** Full-width background image with bold headline ("Your Reliable Worldwide Airport Transfers") and a single tagline underneath. Booking form lives right in the hero.
- **Credential bar:** Three big numbers displayed horizontally — "100+ Countries", "2,100+ Cities", "670+ Airports" — instant scale and trust.
- **How it works section:** Numbered 3-step visual (Enter route → Complete booking → Meet your driver). Short, scannable, removes fear of the unknown.
- **Vehicle class selector:** Clean image cards for each vehicle type with passenger + luggage capacity icons.
- **Email capture:** Newsletter signup offering 5% off first ride — lead gen built into the page flow.
- **Footer trust signals:** All major payment methods shown (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, Google Pay).

### What to steal for our site
- The numbered 3-step "how it works" section
- Credential/scale numbers displayed prominently
- Clean vehicle/service card layout
- Payment icons in the footer

## 2. Kiwitaxi — The Modern + Warm Play
**URL:** https://kiwitaxi.com/

### Why we like it
The most modern and polished design in the space. Feels warm and human rather than corporate. Great balance of bold claims and authentic social proof.

### Key layout elements
- **Hero headline:** "Airport transfers. Simpler than ever." — five words, zero fluff. Immediately followed by two credibility stats (1,400,000+ completed rides, 100+ countries).
- **Animated benefit cards:** Three cards that cycle through the customer journey — Easy Booking → Seamless Meet & Greet → Relaxed and Safe Ride. Emotional, not just functional.
- **Feature deep-dive section:** Each benefit from above expanded into a short paragraph. Walks the customer through what to expect step by step.
- **Reviews section:** Real driver names, real destination cities, real dates — feels more authentic than generic star ratings. Pulls from TripAdvisor and Reviews.co.uk.
- **Award badges:** TripAdvisor Travellers' Choice 2025 + International Travel Awards 2025 placed mid-page.
- **Car class cards:** Visual selector with images of actual vehicles, passenger count, and luggage icons.
- **Additional services section:** Icons for child seats, ski equipment, pets, water, extra stops — upsell presented cleanly without feeling pushy.

### What to steal for our site
- The punchy 5-word headline format
- Animated/cycling benefit cards for the hero section
- Reviews with specific driver names and route details (e.g. "Vancouver to Whistler — John was great")
- Award badge placement mid-page
- The warm, story-like tone throughout

---

## Design Direction Summary

Both sites share the same core DNA — clean layout, bold headline, credential numbers upfront, simple 3-step booking flow — but each has a slightly different personality:

- **Transfeero** = professional, trustworthy, enterprise-feeling
- **Kiwitaxi** = modern, warm, story-driven

For the Whistler Night Shuttle, we want to lean closer to **Kiwitaxi's warmth** (it fits the mountain/adventure vibe) while borrowing **Transfeero's credential structure** for the trust-building sections. 

The night service angle is our unique differentiator — neither of these sites owns that positioning, which is our opportunity.
